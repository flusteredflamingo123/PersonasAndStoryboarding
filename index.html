Personas and Storyboarding

Project Overview

The task: Observe users interacting with a publicly-visible interface. Interview users about their experience and use the data collected to generate two personas and a storyboard representing users of the interface.

The interface: A beverage vending machine, sketched below:

This vending machine interface is designed for users to buy a beverage of their choice with a variety of payment methods. To get a drink, users must pay using the right panel and then press the button below their selection.

Collecting Data

Observations: I observed three users of the machine, which yielded the following key results:

  • One individual pressed the buttons under multiple items and looked at the display showing the price of the item.
  • One individual attempted repeatedly to pay with cash, turning a dollar bill over and rotating it repeatedly before determining that part of the machine was broken.
  • Excluding the events above, all users performed the correct sequence of steps to obtain a drink from the machine without any real hesitation. There were no other signs of confusion or frustration.

Interview: I asked the observed users the following questions after they interacted with the interface:

  1. Did the interface make obvious the steps needed to operate the machine?
  2. Did this vending machine’s interface match your expectation of how a vending machine should be operated? In what ways, if any, did this interface differ from your previous experiences?
  3. Did you face any challenges when interacting with the machine that could be solved with a better interface?
Here is a summary of the users' responses:
  • Users felt that this interface was similar to vending machine interfaces they’ve encountered in the past, making it clear to them what steps were needed to receive their drink. Some users mentioned that they’ve used the exact machine many times before, but the process was still intuitive to a user who had not.
  • Potential interface improvements mentioned:
    • The user who attempted to pay with cash felt that the machine could alert the customer when a payment option was not operational.
    • The option to pay with Brown card funds could be indicated better.
    • Another user said the drink they received looked slightly different than the image displayed on the machine, despite being the correct drink. While this was inconsequential to them, they thought that the actual contents of the machine could be shown to users so they know exactly what to expect.

Personas

From the data gathered above, I constructed two personas representing some of the kinds of users who interact with the interface.

This user is new to campus and thus has not used this machine before. Due to their lack of previous experience, they are impacted by any unclear or broken elements of the interface. This persona reflects the potential difficulties and frustrations that new users face.

This user buys a beverage from the same vending machine on a regular basis. Getting a drink could be a small step in a busy day, so the interface should enable them to complete this task quickly. This persona represents the many users who are returning customers to the vending machine and are already aware of how the interface operates.

Storyboard

Finally, I developed a storyboard illustrating the experience of interacting with the vending machine interface for users like the first-year student persona above.

Takeaways

  • Simply observing users interact with an interface can illuminate flaws in its design.
  • Constructing personas to represent different kinds of users helps reveal how an interface captures or fails to capture the needs of various people.
  • The process of storyboarding can further highlight the essential components of each kind of user's experience with the interface.
